About Reppy

Most people track workouts in a Notes app: quick lists, inconsistent formats, and no easy way to see progress over time.

Reppy is a free gym workout tracker for iOS, built for anyone who wants a structured, smarter place to log sessions and improve. Log exercises, sets, reps, and weight; see stats and trends; and stay consistent with streaks and achievements, without giving up the simplicity you liked in Notes.

Your gym buddies

Report a bug

If something doesn’t look right (a crash, wrong numbers, or a screen that freezes), send us an email with:

  • What you were trying to do
  • What happened instead
  • Your iPhone model and iOS version (Settings → General → About)
  • Screenshots if they help

The more detail you share, the faster we can reproduce and fix it.

FAQ

Is Reppy free?
Yes. Reppy is free to download and use.
What makes it better than Notes?
Reppy is built for workouts: consistent logging, progress and stats, plate math, streaks, and achievements, so you spend less time formatting notes and more time training.
How do I track workouts?
Open the app, start or choose a workout, add exercises, and enter sets with reps and weight. You can build routines over time and review past sessions from your history.
How does progressive overload work?
Progressive overload means gradually increasing demand on your muscles: more weight, reps, or volume over time. Reppy helps you see trends so you know when you’re moving forward and where to push next.
How do I use the plate calculator?
Use the plate calculator when you need to load a target weight on a bar. Enter the weight (and bar type if the app asks), and Reppy suggests plate combinations so you don’t do the math mid-set.
How do I report a bug?
Email reppysupport@gmail.com with what happened, steps to reproduce, and your device details. Screenshots help a lot.
Can I request a feature?
Yes. Use Request a Feature inside the app, or email us with your idea. We can’t promise every request, but we use feedback to prioritize what we build next.
